How to Make Bánh Cuốn: A Step by Step Guide

Instructions

Making bánh cuốn is not difficult but is done in multiple steps. Be sure to prepare the pork filling first so it has time to cool as you make the batter. Before you know it, you'll have a delicious home-cooked meal ready for you and your loved ones!

For the pork filling:

  1. Marinate the ground pork with the stock poweder, salt, suguar and pepper for 15 minutes.
  2. In a large pan, heat up vegetable oil on medium-high heat.
  3. Add the garlic and onion. Saute until fragrant (1-2 minutes).
  4. Add the wood ear mushroom. Be careful as the wood ear mushroom may pop in the pan. Cook for about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the seasoned ground pork . Break up the chucks of ground pork with a wooden spoon. Continue cooking until pork is cooked all the way through

For the batter:

  1. Add all the batter ingredients (except oil) into a medium-size bowl.
  2. Stir until dissolved
  3. Add vegetable oil and stir. The oil will float on top which is okay!

Making the steam rolls:

  1. Preheat a non-stick pan on medium heat. Be sure that you have a lid for this pan.
  2. Pour enough batter into the hot pan to cover the bottom with a thin layer. You wantit as thing as possible. If it is a really good non-stick pan, no oil will be needed.
  3. Cover the pan with the lid and let it steam for about one minute.
  4. Once cooked, flip over the pan to move the "crepe" onto a cutting board.
  5. Add a two tablespoons of the pork mixture in the middle of the "crepe".
  6. Fold the top side towards the middle and then the bottom side up towards the middle.
  7. Repeat until all batter and filling are gone.

For serving:

  1. Slater the rice rolls with a bit of scallion oil.
  2. Top off rolls with cucumbers, been sprouts, herbs, and friend shallots.
  3. Add some slices of pork sausages.
  4. Have a small bowl of Vietnamese fish sauce on the side
ENJOY!
